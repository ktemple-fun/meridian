Most Common Repair

Broken Garage Door Spring Repair

A broken torsion or extension spring is the most common reason a garage door won't open. When a spring snaps, the door becomes too heavy for the opener to lift — and it's dangerous to operate manually.

We stock springs for all residential garage door sizes and can typically complete a spring replacement in under an hour. Don't attempt to replace springs yourself — they're under extreme tension and cause serious injuries when handled incorrectly.

  • Torsion spring replacement (single and double)
  • Extension spring replacement
  • Spring tension adjustment
  • Full spring system inspection

Safety Critical

Garage Door Cable Repair & Replacement

Garage door cables work alongside the springs to lift and lower your door evenly. When a cable snaps or frays, your door can become crooked, get stuck, or fall suddenly — which is a real safety hazard.

If you notice your door is hanging at an angle or one side is lower than the other, a cable is likely the culprit. We'll replace your cables and do a full safety inspection before we leave.

  • Broken cable replacement (both sides)
  • Frayed cable inspection and repair
  • Cable drum and pulley inspection
  • Full balance check after repair

Door hanging crooked?
That's usually a cable. Don't run the opener — call us first.
